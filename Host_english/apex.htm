Nowadays, the big phenomenon in the software development market worldwide is, of course, low-code and no-code platforms. It is these platforms that very dramatically reduce the cost of hiring programmers for any government organization or private company. Simply put, a company manager, instead of paying for many hours of programmer's work, can cobble together for himself many of the solutions he needs in the application he is using, on the interface. What can be clickable in a low-code platform creates a particular look for your website or application, as the manager intended. On the other hand, those issues that cannot be clickable, the PL/SQL and JavaScript MLE language technology developer will program with his own code. Ultimately, however, the number of hours a programmer must work is generally 80 percent less than in an organization that does not use low-code platforms.

And similar to the situation in the cloud market, Oracle Corporation, competes quite well in the market for low-code and non-code platforms. The most popular low-code platforms are Microsoft Power Apps, Wix.com and Bubble. On the other hand, above them all, it is Oracle Application Express that dominates. Oracle APEX, is the most competitive platform and in terms of price and cost. And in terms of product quality. If only because APEX runs on the Oracle Enterprise 23ai database engine, equipped with artificial intelligence. Where Oracle databases, in turn, also dominate the database vendor market.
